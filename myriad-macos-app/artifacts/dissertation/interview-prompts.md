# Appendix A: Interview and Stakeholder Prompt Pack

Use this prompt set to document analysis evidence and sampling rationale.

## A1. Participant Background

1. What is your current study/work context and daily digital routine?
2. Which devices do you use most for messaging, browsing, and social media?
3. What is your current strategy (if any) for managing distracting digital habits?

## A2. Problem Framing

1. Which digital habits feel most difficult to control, and why?
2. At what times or in which contexts do these habits usually occur?
3. What consequences do you notice (focus, stress, productivity, sleep)?

## A3. Existing Solution Experience

1. Have you used screen-time or habit apps before?
2. What worked well in those tools?
3. What felt missing (e.g., guidance, privacy, actionability)?

## A4. Privacy and Trust

1. What data would you be comfortable tracking locally?
2. What data would you not want uploaded to cloud systems?
3. How important are consent control, export, and deletion features to you?

## A5. Feature Prioritization

1. Which feature is most important: analytics visibility, intervention prompts, or privacy controls?
2. Would category-based daily limits be useful? Why or why not?
3. What type of intervention reminders would be acceptable vs. intrusive?

## A6. Prototype/Artefact Evaluation

1. Was the import process understandable?
2. Was goal creation straightforward?
3. Were intervention recommendations specific enough to act on?
4. What one change would most improve the system?

## A7. Sampling Justification Template

- Target group: students with frequent cross-device digital activity.
- Inclusion criteria: uses messaging + browsing daily, willing to complete three guided tasks.
- Exclusion criteria: no regular digital habit concerns, unavailable for 20-30 minute session.
- Sampling strategy: purposive convenience sampling for exploratory formative evaluation.
- Limitation statement: small, non-random sample used for iterative design evidence, not population inference.
